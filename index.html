While help is on the way

Immediate steps you can take

  • 1

    Ensure scene safety

    Move the animal away from traffic, crowds, or aggressive animals. Use a blanket or board to create a barrier.

  • 2

    Check breathing & bleeding

    If there is bleeding, apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth. Do not use tourniquets unless guided by a vet.

  • 3

    Keep them warm & calm

    Use a towel or soft cloth to cover the animal. Speak softly, avoid sudden movements, and never offer food or water to unconscious animals.

  • 4

    Document & share

    Take photos/video, note exact location, and send to the helpline. This speeds up the rescue team.

Basic care & meds

What you can safely do

Minor wounds / abrasions

Clean gently with saline or clean water. Apply povidone-iodine (Betadine) diluted 1:10. Do not use human antiseptics containing alcohol.

Heat stroke / dehydration

Move to shade, place cool (not icy) cloths on paws and belly. Offer ORS solution sip by sip if animal is conscious.

Pain relief

Never self-administer painkillers like paracetamol or ibuprofen. Only use vet-prescribed medicines such as Meloxicam (0.2 mg/kg) after telephonic vet guidance.

When in doubt

If unsure, do nothing invasive. Keep the animal safe and wait for professional help. Incorrect treatment can worsen injuries.

Volunteer, foster, donate

Every helpline listed relies on community support. Offer transport, temporary shelter, or donate medical supplies. Follow up with the NGO after the rescue to ensure continuity of care.
